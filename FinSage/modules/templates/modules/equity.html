Private Equity

Understanding private equity

Private equity is a type of investment that involves buying and investing in private companies. Private companies are companies that are not traded on public stock exchanges. This means that their shares are not available to the general public.

Private equity fund structure

A private equity fund is a type of investment fund that invests in private companies. It is typically structured as a limited partnership, with the general partner (GP) managing the fund and the limited partners (LPs) providing the capital. The GP is responsible for making investment decisions and managing the fund's portfolio, while the LPs are passive investors who do not have any control over the fund's operations.
